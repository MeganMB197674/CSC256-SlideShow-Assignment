Sedona ... McDonald's? (Yum!)

You may be wondering why these McDonald's arches aren't gold. Well, according to the internet and Sedona locals, the classic golden color 'clashes' with the natural red tone of the rocks here. Because of this, the local Sedona McDonald's chose to use turquois for their arches! Because of this odd color choice, the Sedona McDonald's has become a popular tourist destination. Funny, isn't it?

Fun Facts!

  • This McDonald's may be unique, but it isn't the only one! A McDonald's in New Mexico, not too far from Arizona, has a building in the shape of a UFO!

Organ Pipe Cactus

This photo of an Organ Pipe Cactus was taken at the Sonoran Desert Museum in Tucson, Arizona. When we think of cacti, we normally think of the famous Saguaro cactus you see on television all the time, featuring a straight main stalk and arms that reach for the sky. Unlike the Saguaro cactus, the Organ Pipe looks more like an octopus.

Fun Facts!

  • "Organ Pipe Cactus can live to over 150 years in age, and will only produce their first flower near the age of 35." (NPS.gov, 2015)
  • Organ Pipe Cacti bloom white flowers that only stay open at night, which allows bats to do most of the pollination. (NPS.gov, 2015)
  • "Sub-freezing temperatures will kill young tissue at the end of the stems. When growth begins again, the results are indentations, or the appearance of circular waves on the organ pipes." (NPS.gov, 2015)
  • The main area in the country that these cacti grow is in the Organ Pipe National Monument of Arizona, which houses the perfect climate and geological setting. (NPS.gov, 2015)

The Grand Canyon: South Rim Lookout

This photo was taken in March of 2019 at the south rim lookout inside of the Grand Canyon National Park. People can be seen standing on a nearby cliff - but the interesting part is - this cliff has no railing, as it is NOT part of the designated lookout site. I was in some shock taking this, as many people have fallen off of the canyon cliffs according to park rangers. Nonetheless, it is a beautiful sight, with rock colors ranging from the tan and beige tone seen in this photo, to green plant life, to crimson and brown.

Fun (and Some Brutal) Facts!

  • "On average, two to three deaths per year are from accidental falls over the rim, park spokeswoman Kirby-Lynn Shedlowski says." (Wadzinski, 2023)
  • The grand Canyon was carved over 6 million years ago by geological activity and the Colorado River, which runs through the bottom of the canyon. (U.S. Department of the Interior, 2021)
  • "An estimated 5.9 million people visit the Grand Canyon a year, making it the second most popular national park following just behind the Great Smoky Mountains of North Carolina and Tennessee." (U.S. Department of the Interior, 2021)
  • The most dangerous animal in the park is a squirrel? Yes, you read that right. The Rock Squirrel is known to attack dozens of visitors that attempt to feed these cute little mammals. (U.S. Department of the Interior, 2021)

Papillon Helicopter Tours

This photo was taken moments before my father and I took flight on a Papillon Helicopter tour! This tour took us over the south rim of the Grand Canyon, allowing us to view buffalo, rams, plant life, rock formations, caves, and the Colorado River! It is a much needed adventure that I recommend everyone take at some point if they can. The tour lasted around 30 minutes. During the tour, passengers wear headphones equipped with microphones that allow them to speak with one another and the pilot. Although, I think that the best part of the tour was the song "What a Wonderful World" by Louis Armstrong playing as we went over the canyon. Needless to say, everyone cried a bit. Link to Papillon Tours page

Fun Facts!

  • According to the pilots of the Papillon tours, there is an albino buffalo roaming the canyon. It is highly protected by the park rangers.
  • The temperature in the winter and spring seasons can drop extremely low, oftentimes covering the canyon with frost and snow. This makes for a beautiful sight atop of the red rock.
  • If you decide to take one of these helicopter tours, they are offered in increments of 15 minutes and allow you to choose from the south rim of the canyon, north rim (which is more tan and closer to the Hoover Dam), and into Las Vegas as well.

The Crater Bowl

The crater bowl is truly amazing, and large. The crater is said to be 4,000 feet in diameter and is one of the best preserved and well-known meteor strike locations in the world. It is also said to emulate the surface of the moon in appearance and texture.

Fun Facts!

  • "On July 20, 1969, the three Apollo 11 astronauts ‚Äî Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, and Michael Collins, took smaller steps for mankind, using Meteor Crater as their key training site." (MeteorCrater.com, 2020)
  • The crater was discovered in 1891, and its age has been variously estimated at between 5,000 and 50,000 years. (The Editors of Encyclopedia Britannica, 2020)

The Meteor Responsible!

This is the star of the show, the cause of it all, and a very interesting rock! (Believe it or not). This meteor, comprised of iron, struck the earth hard enough to be compared to the force of a nuclear blast and excavated 175 million tons of rock upon impact. (Kring & Bailey, 2023)

Fun Facts!

  • A meteor strike such as this one occurs roughly every 6000 years. (Kring & Bailey, 2022)
  • "The explosive shockwave would have produced winds in excess of 1000 km/hr within 3 to 5 km of the impact!" (Kring & Bailey, 2022)
